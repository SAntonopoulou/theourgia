# Practice

The practice pages are the daily engine of the vault: a tracker for
the practices you have committed to, quick logs for dreams,
pathworkings, body work, and banishings, entry templates for the
longer records, and a recipe book for what you blend and brew.

## The daily practice tracker

The tracker at `/daily-practice` holds the practices you perform on a
cadence — meditation, adorations, offerings, whatever you have
committed to. Define a practice once, then each day mark it **done**
or **skipped** with one press. The tracker keeps your streak for each
practice and reconciles counts with the vault on every refresh.

Practices can be archived when you retire them and unarchived if you
return; nothing is deleted. A Liber Resh reference card sits alongside
the tracker for magicians keeping the solar adorations — see
[Calendars and the sky](calendars-and-sky.md) for the full Resh
support.

The tracker counts what you asked it to count and nothing else. There
is no guilt mechanic; a skipped day is recorded as a skip, not a
failure banner.

## Practice logs

The Practice log page (`/practice-logs`) is a quick-entry surface with
four tabs.

### Dream

A fast dream capture — write it before it fades. Saving creates a
dream entry in your journal titled with the date, with a lucid marker
and symbol and figure chips. Dream symbols share an index with the
scrying log, so a symbol that recurs across dreams and visions can be
found in one search. For the very fastest capture on waking, the
full-screen `/capture` page also works and can be sorted later.

### Pathworking

A per-path journey log for Tree of Life work. Choose your tradition —
Lurianic, Golden Dawn, or Thelemic — and the vault supplies the path
metadata (path number, Hebrew letter, tarot trump, and the route
between spheres). Saving creates a pathworking entry carrying that
metadata alongside your journey narrative.

### Asana

Body practice sessions in the Liber E style: asana, pranayama, or
other, with duration. The vault keeps cumulative totals per practice
kind, so "how many hours have I actually sat" has an answer.

### Banishing

A quick log for banishing, grounding, and centering: pick the rite —
Lesser Banishing Ritual of the Pentagram, Star Ruby, simple grounding,
breath, water, salt, bell, incense, Khephra (solar invocation in lieu
of explicit banishing), or other — set the time, add a note. The vault
tracks your banishing cadence.

One honesty note: the Banishing tab offers a seal toggle, but at
present it records a sealed *flag* while the text itself is stored
unencrypted — and the surface tells you exactly that whenever you use
it. If a banishing record needs a true seal today, write it as a
journal entry and seal it there.

## Entry templates

The Templates page (`/templates`) starts an entry from a tested
structure. Twelve built-in templates ship with the vault:

Magical Record (Crowley), Ritual Log, Dream, Divination,
Synchronicity, Liber Resh adoration, Banishing, Invocation, Scrying,
Tarot Reading, Pathworking, and Astrology Reading.

Each template pre-fills a title pattern (for example
"Ritual — {date}") and the entry's structure. You can also create,
edit, and retire your own personal templates.

## Ritual templates and working records

Longer ritual work is recorded in the journal with two entry kinds:
a **ritual log** for the performance of a rite, and a **working** for
an operation pursued over time. The Ritual Log and Invocation
templates give both a starting structure, and the `/ritual` slash
command drops a ritual log block into any entry. Beings invoked are
linked with `/entity` references, so a working's history shows up on
the entity's side too.

## Body sensation diagrams

The `/sensation` slash command inserts a body sensation diagram into
any entry: a body silhouette on which you place labelled points for
what you felt and where. Sensation diagrams are how body data enters
the record — energy movements during ritual, sensations in trance,
somatic responses to a working.

## Recipes

The recipe book at `/recipes` holds your formulations: incense, oils,
washes, philtres, and anything else. A recipe carries its ingredient
list, its preparation steps, and its correspondences, and can link to
the books in your library it came from and the beings it is
compounded for. Kind chips filter the collection.
